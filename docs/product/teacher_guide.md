# Teacher Guide — EduBoost

**Last Updated**: 2026-06-12  
**Audience**: Teachers using EduBoost to monitor learner progress

---

## Introduction

EduBoost helps teachers track learner progress, identify gaps, and support student success. This guide explains how to use EduBoost effectively in your classroom.

---

## Getting Started

### Teacher Account Setup

1. Your school administrator will invite you via email
2. Click the invitation link
3. Create your password and complete your profile
4. Log in at [eduboost.co.za](https://eduboost.co.za)

### Linking Learners to Your Class

Learners must be assigned to your class by an administrator. Once assigned:

1. Go to **My Class** → **Learners**
2. View your assigned learners
3. Each learner's progress is visible to you

> **Note**: You can only see learners assigned to your class. You cannot add learners yourself — contact your school administrator for changes.

---

## Dashboard Overview

### Class Summary

The teacher dashboard shows:

- **Class Progress**: Average mastery score across all assigned learners
- **Active Learners**: Number of learners who logged in this week
- **Top Topics**: Most mastered topics in your class
- **Areas Needing Attention**: Topics where many learners have low mastery

### Individual Learner View

Click any learner to see:

- **Progress Summary**: XP earned, lessons completed, current streak
- **Topic Mastery**: Detailed breakdown by CAPS topic
- **Diagnostic History**: Past assessment scores
- **Activity Log**: Recent lesson activity

---

## Understanding Learner Data

### Mastery Scores

Each topic has a mastery score from 0–100%:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0–40% | Beginning — needs significant support |
| 41–70% | Developing — making progress |
| 71–90% | Proficient — solid understanding |
| 91–100% | Mastered — complete understanding |

### Diagnostic Results

Diagnostic assessments use Item Response Theory (IRT) to estimate learner ability. Scores are expressed as logits on a continuous scale.

### Progress Over Time

View progress charts showing:

- Weekly XP trends
- Topic mastery over time
- Diagnostic score progression

## Creating Reports

### Class Report

Generate a PDF report for your class:

1. Go to **Reports** → **Class Report**
2. Select date range
3. Choose topics to include
4. Click **Generate PDF**

### Individual Learner Report

1. Go to **My Class** → select learner
2. Click **Generate Report**
3. Choose format: **PDF** or **CSV**
4. Download or email

## Integrating with Teaching

### Using Data to Guide Instruction

- **Identify gaps**: Review topics with low class mastery
- **Target interventions**: Focus on learners with the lowest scores
- **Celebrate progress**: Share top performers and streak leaders

### Suggested Workflow

1. **Weekly Review**: Log in every Friday to check class progress
2. **Monthly Report**: Generate a class report for your records
3. **Term Planning**: Use diagnostic data to plan intervention groups

---

## Limitations

As a teacher, you can:

- ✅ View learner progress
- ✅ Generate reports
- ✅ View diagnostic results

You cannot:

- ❌ Modify learner accounts
- ❌ Change learner consent settings
- ❌ Access billing information
- ❌ Add or remove learners (contact administrator)

---

## Troubleshooting

### I don't see any learners

- Check with your administrator to ensure learners are assigned to your class
- Verify your email is correct in the system

### Data looks outdated

- Data refreshes every hour
- Check back later for latest numbers

### Cannot generate report

- Ensure at least one learner is assigned to your class
- Try a different date range

## Best Practices

1. **Check weekly**: Regular monitoring helps catch struggling learners early
2. **Share progress**: Include EduBoost data in parent meetings
3. **Use reports**: PDF reports are useful for records and meetings
4. **Encourage streaks**: Motivate learners to log in daily
